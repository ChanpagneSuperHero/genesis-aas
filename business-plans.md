## AI SaaS Unit Economics (2026 Reality)

Traditional SaaS enjoyed 78-85% gross margins. AI-first companies face a fundamentally different cost structure:

| Metric | Traditional SaaS | AI-First SaaS (2026) |
|--------|------------------|----------------------|
| Gross Margin | 78-85% | 55-70% |
| Variable COGS per User | <5% of revenue | 20-40% of revenue |
| Infrastructure % of Revenue | 8-12% | 25-40% |
| Marginal Cost per Transaction | Near-zero | $0.01-$0.50+ |

**Current inference costs (declining ~78% YoY for some providers):**
- GPT-4 Turbo: $10-30/1M input tokens, $30-60/1M output tokens
- Claude 3 Opus: $15/1M input, $75/1M output
- Self-hosted Llama 3 70B: $0.50-2/1M tokens + infrastructure
- Fine-tuning: $5K-50K per model iteration
- Vector databases (RAG): $0.10-0.50/GB/month

**Critical insight:** Inference costs are falling fast. What costs $1 today may cost $0.22 in a year. Genesis should architect for this deflation — margins improve automatically over time.

**Benchmark unit economics for AI agent businesses:**
- Target gross margin: 60-70% (improving to 75%+ as costs decline)
- CAC for consumer: $5-30 (organic/PLG) to $50-150 (paid)
- LTV target: 3-5x CAC minimum
- Monthly churn target: <5% (consumer), <2% (prosumer/business)
- Average cost per active user per month: $2-8 (heavy agent usage)

# Strategic Frameworks

## Diamandis Exponential Organization (ExO) Framework

Peter Diamandis and Salim Ismail's framework identifies 11 ExO Attributes — the shared DNA of organizations that achieve 10x impact:

### The Six Ds of Exponentials
1. **Digitization** — Once something becomes digital, it enters exponential growth
2. **Deception** — Early exponential growth looks flat (0.01, 0.02, 0.04...)
3. **Disruption** — The exponential curve breaks through and disrupts incumbents
4. **Demonetization** — The cost approaches zero (AI inference costs falling 78%/year)
5. **Dematerialization** — Physical products become apps and services
6. **Democratization** — Once free/cheap, it becomes available to everyone

### Massive Transformative Purpose (MTP)
The single most important attribute of an ExO. It must be:
- **Massive** — Aims to change the world
- **Transformative** — Fundamentally shifts how things work
- **Purpose** — Not a mission statement — a reason for being

**Genesis MTP: "A digital partner for every human on Earth."**

This is a perfect ExO MTP — massive (8B people), transformative (intelligence augmentation), purpose-driven (empowerment, not extraction).

### ExO Building Steps (Diamandis)
1. Select your Massive Transformative Purpose ✓
2. Join or create communities around your MTP
3. Compose a team (humans + AI)
4. Identify a Moonshot (breakthrough idea)
5. Build with exponential technologies
6. Launch, iterate, scale

## Saylor's Platform & Network Effects Principles

Michael Saylor's strategic thinking (from *The Mobile Wave* and MicroStrategy's evolution):

1. **Platform over product** — Don't build a tool; build the platform tools are built on
2. **Network effects compound** — Every new user makes the network more valuable for all users
3. **Digital transformation is inevitable** — Bet on digitization of everything
4. **Smart leverage** — Use available capital instruments to amplify position
5. **First-mover conviction** — When you see the wave, commit fully; half-measures fail
6. **Software is eating the world** — The dominant digital platform in any category captures most of the value

**Application to Genesis:** Don't build "an AI assistant." Build the platform where emergent intelligence partnerships form and grow. The intelligence layer is the new mobile wave.

## The Genesis Strategic Synthesis

Combining Diamandis + Saylor + PLG playbooks:

1. **MTP attracts community** (Diamandis) → Community creates content and evangelism for free
2. **Platform creates lock-in** (Saylor) → Your AI partner knows you; switching costs are enormous
3. **Freemium creates virality** (Slack/Canva) → Free tier so good people can't stop talking about it
4. **Demonetization creates moat** (Diamandis) → As AI costs fall, Genesis margins expand while competitors still charge enterprise prices
5. **Network effects compound** (Saylor) → Shared intelligence, templates, and integrations make the platform more valuable per user
